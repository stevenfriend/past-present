Past Tense: Regular Verbs

There are two kinds of verbs regular verbs and irregular verbs. Regular verbs have a rule when we make them past tense, we add ‘ed’ to the end of the word:


動詞の原形 + ed

walk ⇒ walked


Note: If a regular verb ends with consonant + y, we change ‘y’ to ‘i’ then add ‘ed’. Remember that the vowels are; a, e, i, o, u. Consonants are the other letters.


study ⇒ studied

play ⇒ played

The ‘ed’ added to the end of each verb can be pronounced three different ways:


/t/ /d/ /id/


Consonants can be voiced or unvoiced. Voiced consonants make your vocal cords vibrate when you say them. For example, try putting you hand on your throat and making the sounds /b/, /g/ and /z/. Unvoiced sounds do not vibrate the vocal cords in this way. For example, /f/, /p/ and /s/.

If the simple present tense verb ends with an unvoiced sound, we pronounce the ‘ed’ as /t/:


原形 過去形 意味
cook cooked 料理した
watch watched 見た
wash washed 洗った
walk walked 歩いた
practice practiced 練習した

If the simple present tense verb ends with a voiced sound, we pronounce the ‘ed’ as /d/.


原形 過去形 意味
listen listened 聞いた
clean cleaned 掃除した
play played 遊んだ
study studied 勉強した
cry cried 泣いた

If the simple present tense verb ends with ‘d’ or ‘t’, we pronounce the ‘ed’ as /id/.


原形 過去形 意味
vist visited 訪問した
want wanted ほしかった
start started 始めた
end ended 終わった
need needed 必要だった
